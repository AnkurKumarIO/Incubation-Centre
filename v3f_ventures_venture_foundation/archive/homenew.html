About V3F Nagpur

Nurturing the Spirit of Entrepreneurship

At the heart of innovation lies the spirit of entrepreneurship — and V3F Nagpur is committed to nurturing this spirit. As VNIT's incubation wing, we catalyse startups that drive positive change, innovation, and economic development across the region.

Our Mission

Building a Founder-First Ecosystem

V3F Nagpur provides access to state-of-the-art facilities, a network of mentors, industry connections, and a supportive community that empowers startups to thrive. We aim to create an environment that encourages sustainable entrepreneurship and long-term ecosystem development.

Incubated startups gain access to the broader VNIT innovation ecosystem — including faculty expertise, research infrastructure, student talent, industry partnerships, and investor networks.

✅ VNIT Affiliation: V3F operates as a Section 8 company affiliated with VNIT Nagpur, with the Director of VNIT serving as Chairman of the Board of Directors.

Governance Structure

Transparency & Accountability

V3F Nagpur is incorporated as a Section 8 company under the Companies Act, affiliated with VNIT Nagpur. While we maintain operational autonomy for fast decision-making, we remain deeply aligned with VNIT's institutional vision.

  • The Director of VNIT Nagpur chairs the Board of Directors
  • Operational activities are reported to the Board quarterly
  • Policy revisions are made periodically by the Governing Board
  • Faculty startup and IPR matters are guided by VNIT policies
  • Disputes are resolved through mediation or arbitration
  • All startups submit audited financial statements annually

Selection Authority

Final incubation decisions rest with the V3F Governing Board or an authorised committee.

IP Ownership

IP created using VNIT resources is governed by the VNIT IPR Policy.

Exit Strategy

V3F exits equity via founder buyback, secondary share sale, or acquisition — timing determined by the Governing Board.

How It Works

Mentor–Startup Matching Process

1

Onboarding & Profiling

On joining V3F, each startup completes a profiling session covering their sector, stage, key challenges, and mentorship needs.

2

Tailored Matching

V3F matches startups with mentors from the pool based on sector alignment, stage of development, and specific support requirements.

3

Logged Sessions

All mentoring sessions are logged and documented for record keeping and impact assessment. Both parties submit brief post-session feedback.

4

Annual Review

Mentors are onboarded for one year, with extensions based on performance and feedback from startups and V3F management.

What Happens Next

Selection Process

Step 1
Application Submission
Submit your application with supporting documents. V3F acknowledges all applications within 48 hours.
Step 2
Initial Screening
V3F management reviews applications against the evaluation matrix — innovation, market potential, team capability, and strategic fit.
Step 3
Shortlisting
Shortlisted startups are invited for a presentation before the Selection Committee comprising V3F management, VNIT faculty, investors, and industry experts.
Step 4
Governing Board Approval
Final incubation decisions rest with the V3F Governing Board or an authorised committee constituted by the Board.
Step 5
Onboarding
Selected startups sign the incubation agreement, complete documentation, attend orientation, and define quarterly milestones. ID cards are issued for facility access.

Timeline

Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. Expect 4–6 weeks from submission to final decision.

Quarterly Cohorts

V3F runs quarterly intake cycles. Apply early to be considered for the next cohort.

🏛️ About V3F Nagpur
What is V3F Nagpur? ▼
V3F Nagpur (VNIT Foundation for Startups) is the official incubation centre of Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology (VNIT), Nagpur. It is a Section 8 company that supports early-stage startups and entrepreneurs through co-working space, mentorship, funding guidance, and access to VNIT's world-class research infrastructure.
Is V3F Nagpur affiliated with VNIT? ▼
Yes. V3F Nagpur is VNIT's official incubation vehicle — incorporated as a Section 8 (non-profit) company under the Companies Act 2013. It operates within the VNIT Nagpur campus on South Ambazari Road, giving incubated startups direct access to VNIT's labs, faculty, and student talent.
What types of startups does V3F support? ▼
V3F focuses on technology-driven startups across sectors such as Deep Tech, AI & Digital Technologies, Manufacturing & Engineering, Clean Energy & Sustainability, Healthcare Technologies, AgriTech, and other sectors aligned with national innovation priorities. Both hardware and software ventures are welcome.
Do I need to be a VNIT student or alumnus to apply? ▼
No. While V3F welcomes VNIT students, faculty, and alumni, incubation is open to any entrepreneur or startup team with a relevant technology-focused venture. External founders, industry professionals, and inter-institutional teams are all eligible to apply.
🚀 Programs & Eligibility
What programs does V3F offer? ▼
V3F offers three structured programs: (1) Pre-Incubation — for early-stage ideas and student ventures needing validation support; (2) Incubation — a full-fledged 18–36 month program for startups with a prototype or early traction; (3) Co-Working Membership — flexible workspace access within the VNIT campus without a formal incubation commitment.
What stage should my startup be at to apply for full Incubation? ▼
For the Incubation program, you should ideally have at least a working prototype or minimum viable product (MVP), a defined target market, and a founding team in place. Pure idea-stage ventures are better suited for Pre-Incubation first. However, exceptionally strong teams with compelling ideas may be considered case-by-case.
Does V3F take equity in my startup? ▼
Yes. V3F Nagpur takes a minimum 2% equity stake in incubated startups as consideration for incubation services, infrastructure, and support. This is standard practice among DSIR-recognised incubators in India and ensures alignment between V3F and your startup's success.
How long is the incubation program? ▼
The standard incubation tenure is 18 to 36 months, depending on your startup's stage and goals. This is structured with quarterly milestone reviews. Extensions beyond 36 months may be considered on a case-by-case basis with Governing Board approval.
Can a team of students apply while still enrolled at VNIT or another college? ▼
Yes! Student teams are actively encouraged to apply, especially for Pre-Incubation. V3F is designed to support academic entrepreneurship, and many of the mentors and advisors are alumni or faculty who understand the student founder journey. You may apply as a team even before formally registering a company.
🏢 Facilities & Mentorship
What physical facilities does V3F provide? ▼
Incubated startups get access to co-working desks, private cabins for teams, meeting rooms, high-speed internet, printing facilities, and access to VNIT's research laboratories. The campus also has a canteen, sports facilities, and a library — all within the VNIT campus environment.
How does the mentorship program work? ▼
Each incubated startup is matched with dedicated Mentors based on their sector and stage. Mentors are experienced professionals from industry, academia, and investor networks who provide hands-on guidance. Sessions are typically monthly but can be more frequent during critical phases. Mentors are distinct from V3F's Advisory Board — advisors guide the incubator itself, while mentors work directly with your startup.
Does V3F help with funding? ▼
V3F provides active guidance on government grant schemes (BIRAC, DST, NIDHI, Startup India, etc.), introductions to angel networks such as Deccan Angel Network, and connections with VC investors. V3F does not directly invest in startups but facilitates fundraising through its investor relations and ecosystem connections.
Is legal and IP support available? ▼
Yes. Incubated startups receive guidance on company incorporation, intellectual property (patents, trademarks), legal documentation, and compliance. V3F connects startups with legal and IP experts from its network. VNIT's Technology Transfer Office also supports patent filing for innovations developed in collaboration with VNIT faculty.
📝 Application & Process
How do I apply to V3F Nagpur? ▼
You can apply directly through the Apply page on this website. The application requires details about your startup, founding team, technology, market opportunity, and current stage. Upload a pitch deck and any supporting documents. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis with quarterly cohort cycles.
How long does the selection process take? ▼
Expect 4–6 weeks from application submission to final decision. The process includes: initial screening by V3F management (2 weeks), shortlisting, presentation to the Selection Committee, and final approval by the Governing Board. V3F acknowledges all applications within 48 hours of submission.
What documents do I need to submit? ▼
Required documents include: a pitch deck (PDF), founder ID proofs, company registration certificate (if incorporated), proof of prototype/product (photos, videos, or demo links), any existing patents or IP filings, and financial projections or business plan. The complete document checklist is listed on the Apply page.
Can I apply if my startup is already registered? ▼
Yes, and it's actually preferred. Registered companies (Private Limited, LLP, or OPC) are eligible. If your startup is not yet registered, V3F can assist with incorporation guidance as part of pre-incubation support. You do not need to be registered to initiate an inquiry or begin Pre-Incubation.
Is there a fee to join V3F? ▼
V3F charges a nominal incubation fee that covers workspace, administrative support, and basic services. The exact fee structure depends on the program (Pre-Incubation, Incubation, or Co-Working) and the specific facilities required. Details are shared with shortlisted applicants. The 2% equity arrangement is separate from operational fees.
